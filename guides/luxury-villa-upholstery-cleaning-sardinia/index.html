Guest preparation

What to check before guests arrive

In premium villas, textile surfaces do not need to be disastrous to create a bad impression. They just need to look tired, smell closed or show one visible stain in the wrong place. Hospitality is absurdly fragile like that.

Sofas and lounge seating

Check armrests, back cushions, head contact areas, seat fronts and any light-coloured fabrics.

Dining and accent chairs

Upholstered chairs can show small marks that become very visible in bright rooms.

Cushions and soft furnishings

Loose cushions often collect odours, sunscreen traces, dust and guest-use marks.

Carpets and rugs

Villa carpets and rugs should be checked together with upholstery because odours often move through the room, not by department.

Before booking

Prepare a useful photo assessment

Villa textile cleaning is easier to plan when the room layout, surfaces and timing are clear before the appointment.

1

Photograph the full room

Show sofa, seating, light direction and nearby carpets so the textile context is visible.

2

Add detail photos

Send close-ups of stains, fabric texture, seams, cushions and any high-use areas.

3

Explain the guest schedule

Mention arrival date, turnover window, access, parking and whether the property is already prepared.

Common villa issues

Textile problems that affect guest perception

Closed-room odours

Villas closed for weeks or months can hold odour in upholstery, mattresses, rugs and curtains.

Sunscreen and body oils

Guest areas near pools and terraces often collect residues on sofas and cushions.

Light fabrics

Beige, white and pale upholstery looks premium until real life touches it, which it insists on doing.

Turnover pressure

When guests arrive soon, focus on the surfaces they will see and use first.

Useful photos

Photos to send from the villa

Good photos show both the detail and the room. A stain without context is a clue, not a plan.

FAQ

Questions about luxury villa upholstery cleaning

When should villa upholstery be cleaned before guest arrival?

Ideally before final staging, linen preparation and room fragrance, leaving realistic time for drying and ventilation.

Can I send photos of sofas and chairs?

Yes. Wide room photos plus close-ups of stains and fabric texture are useful for a first assessment.

Do all stains come out?

No. Results depend on fabric, stain cause, age and products already used.

Should I clean upholstery and mattresses together?

Often yes, especially when the issue is odour, closed rooms or full guest preparation.

Do you cover Costa Smeralda villas?

Service can be organised in Costa Smeralda and other Sardinian areas depending on availability and intervention details.
